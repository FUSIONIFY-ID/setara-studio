# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Affluent couples, creative professionals, fashion houses, and luxury brands seeking cinematic photography services. They arrive via word-of-mouth or search, browsing on high-resolution devices in quiet, considered moments -- evaluating whether the studio's aesthetic matches their personal vision before committing to a four-figure booking.

## Product Purpose

LUMIERE is a luxury photography studio marketing site. It exists to convey cinematic artistry, build emotional trust through portfolio presentation, and convert high-intent visitors into private consultation bookings. Success is measured by session reservation requests and perceived brand prestige.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, intimate, luxurious. The studio treats every photograph as an heirloom -- the site should feel like stepping into a private screening room where light, silence, and composition speak. Not loud, not sparse; layered and purposeful.

## Anti-references

- Overly minimalist or sparse sites that feel empty or lack personality
- Generic stock-photography portfolio templates (WordPress themes, cookie-cutter grids)
- Brutalist or stripped-down layouts that undermine the luxury positioning
- Sites that rely on text walls instead of letting imagery carry the story

## Design Principles

1. **Let the image lead** -- Every layout decision defers to the photograph. UI chrome retreats; the frame is the interface.
2. **Earned intimacy** -- Reveal information progressively, like a quiet conversation. The site rewards patience, not urgency.
3. **Cinematic weight** -- Transitions, spacing, and typography carry the tempo of a slow film. Nothing snaps; everything breathes.
4. **Substance over decoration** -- Every element must justify its presence. Ornament is acceptable only when it reinforces the narrative.
5. **Quiet confidence** -- The brand doesn't shout. Authority comes from restraint, craft, and attention to invisible details.

## Accessibility and Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum contrast ratios
- Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations (already implemented via prefers-reduced-motion)
- Keyboard-navigable lightbox and form controls
- Semantic HTML landmarks and proper heading hierarchy
